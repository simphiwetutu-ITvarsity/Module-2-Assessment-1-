GIRAFFES

The giraffe is a long-necked mammal that is native to Africa. They are the tallest land animals, standing up to 18 feet tall, with long legs and a slender body. Giraffes have a distinctive spotted pattern on their fur, which varies in color from light tan to dark brown.

Their long necks allow them to reach leaves and branches that other animals cannot, and they are herbivores that primarily feed on leaves from acacia trees. They have a prehensile tongue that is over a foot long and allows them to grasp branches and strip the leaves from them.

Giraffes are social animals that live in groups called towers or herds, which can consist of up to 20 individuals. They communicate with each other through a variety of vocalizations, body language, and olfactory signals.
