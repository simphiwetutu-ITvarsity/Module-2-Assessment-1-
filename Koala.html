KOALA

The Koala is a small, tree-dwelling marsupial native to Australia. They have a distinctive appearance, with a round head, large furry ears, and a flat nose. Koalas have a thick, grayish-brown fur that helps protect them from the elements and predators.

Koalas are primarily herbivores and feed on eucalyptus leaves, which make up almost their entire diet. They have a unique digestive system that allows them to break down the tough leaves and extract nutrients from them.

Koalas are solitary animals and spend most of their time sleeping in the trees, often in a curled-up position. They are known for their adorable appearance and gentle demeanor, which has made them popular with people around the world.
